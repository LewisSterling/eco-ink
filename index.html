The Background

Artificial inks are designed for performance and cost, not for safety or sustainability. They are:
  • Non-biodegradable
  • Chemically persistent
  • Unregulated in daily student exposure

These issues combine into one systemic problem: everyday chemical exposure is treated as harmless, even though it is not studied as harmless.

Problem Scale

School Level

Daily exposure to pens and whiteboard markers may cause headaches, dizziness, respiratory irritation, and long-term risks such as organ damage and oxygen deprivation.

Community

Ink waste enters soil, wastewater and landfills. Individually small, but massive in accumulation due to billions of users.

Myanmar

Limited regulation, poor wastewater treatment and heavy reliance on imported stationery allow chemical inks to enter ecosystems unchecked.

Research Framework

Research Question

Can eco-friendly inks made from local and natural materials reduce environmental persistence, minimize chemical exposure for students, and remain practical alternatives to common school-use chemical inks?

Hypothesis

Eco-friendly inks made from natural, locally available materials will degrade faster, have lower toxicity and volatile compounds, and maintain comparable writing performance.
